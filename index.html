Portable ADS-B receiver support

Works with open GDL90 Wi‑Fi.

PilotBriefAI connects to any ADS-B receiver that broadcasts standard GDL90 over Wi-Fi on UDP port 4000. Join the receiver's Wi-Fi network, open FLY, and paid plans can show live ownship and traffic on your sectional.

Required spec: standard GDL90 over Wi-Fi, UDP 4000

Fully supported

  • Stratux DIY or pre-built receivers, including CrewDog-style builds.
  • Appareo Stratus 3 / 3i with Open ADS-B Mode enabled in Stratus Horizon Pro.
  • Levil iLevil 3 AW/SW and BOM native GDL90 Wi-Fi receivers.
  • uAvionix echoUAT, skySensor, AV-Link, and SkyEcho 2 where regionally legal and available.

What to look for

  • Receiver documentation says GDL90 output or Open ADS-B mode.
  • It supports third-party EFB apps such as FltPlan Go, FlyQ, WingX, or similar tools.
  • Dynon portable or SkyView Wi-Fi may work, but uses a tweaked GDL90 stream; verify with your unit before relying on it.

Not compatible

  • ForeFlight Sentry, Sentry Plus, and Sentry Mini use a closed ForeFlight protocol.
  • Garmin GDL 50, GDL 52, GDL 39, and GDL 39 3D use Garmin Connext, not open GDL90.
  • Bad Elf units are GPS-only and do not provide ADS-B traffic.

Pilot objections, answered

Built to fit the way pilots already brief weather.

Do I still need ForeFlight or Garmin Pilot?

Yes, and keep them. PilotBriefAI does not replace charts, plates, filing, or certified navigation. FLY mode is a supplemental VFR situational-awareness aid with sectionals, offline chart downloads, weather overlays, live METARs, Direct-To, and ADS-B traffic context. Use your EFB where you need filing, plates, and full chart workflows.

What makes it different from a weather app?

Weather apps and EFBs show conditions. PilotBriefAI uses Claude to interpret those conditions against your personal minimums and aircraft, then adds route PIREPs, live ATC audio, and live ADS-B tracking.

Where does this fit with my EFB workflow?

Use PilotBriefAI before you open the filing workflow: brief the route, review AI-interpreted hazards and PIREPs, decide Go / Marginal / No-Go, then take that decision into ForeFlight or Garmin Pilot for charts, filing, plates, and navigation.

Which ADS-B receivers work with FLY?

Paid plans support any receiver broadcasting standard GDL90 over Wi-Fi on UDP port 4000, including Stratux, Appareo Stratus 3 with Open ADS-B Mode, Levil iLevil, and uAvionix echoUAT / skySensor / AV-Link devices. ForeFlight Sentry and Garmin GDL receivers use closed protocols, and Bad Elf units are GPS-only.

Which AI does it use?

Anthropic's Claude. It reads live FAA/NOAA data server-side and produces the plain-English PAVE analysis and Go / Marginal / No-Go result.

Is this an official weather briefing?

No. It is a situational-awareness aid. Always get an official briefing from an FAA-approved source such as 1800wxbrief.com. The go/no-go decision is always yours as PIC.

How does the free trial work?

14 days of full IFR-tier access, no credit card, one per pilot.

Safety first

Honest about its role.

PilotBriefAI is a situational-awareness aid, NOT a certified weather or flight-planning service. It does not replace an official preflight briefing from an FAA-approved source such as 1800wxbrief.com or Flight Service, and must never be used as your sole source of weather, NOTAM, or flight-planning information. PilotBriefAI does not make the go/no-go decision — it surfaces how live data compares to your personal minimums, in plain English. Every decision, and the safe conduct of the flight, remains solely yours as pilot in command. Weather, NOTAM, TFR, PIREP, and winds-aloft data may be delayed, incomplete, unavailable, or inaccurate, and AI summaries can contain errors. Always verify all safety-critical information against official primary sources before flight, as required by 14 CFR 91.103. You assume all risk of using this app.
